Reporting Misconduct

Policy Number: Effective Date:
12345 01 Aug 2004

Scope

Defining,identifying, and reporting procedures for misconduct.

Duration

Policy is permanent to be reviewed annually.

Policy

Describe the company policy, for example:

encourages all employees to report misconduct. is committed to protecting employees who report suspected misconduct. The term "employee" includes all staff, independent contractors and volunteers.

Confidentiality

will try to prevent disclosure of the identity of the employee reporting alleged misconduct. However, the identity of an employee may become obvious to others due to the nature of the information. The employee's identity will be disclosed:

  • to the law enforcement agency investigating the matter.
  • to employees assigned to investigate the matter.

Misconduct

Misconduct is an activity performed by an employee that violates state and/or federal laws or regulations, local ordinances, or policy. Misconduct includes, but is not limited to, the examples listed below.

  • Alcohol or drug use
  • Bribery
  • Corruption
  • Discrimination
  • Endangerment of health or safety of others
  • Falsification of reports
  • Misuse, mismanagement, or misappropriation of funds, property, facilities or any other assets.
  • Personal use of materials or assets
  • Release of confidential data
  • Sexual harassment
  • Theft of property
  • Willful failure to perform duties
